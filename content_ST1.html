The Importance of Nutrition for Good Health and Well-Being

Introduction

Nutrition is an important factor in maintaining a healthy well-being and is gained through food consumption but the amount of nutrition may vary. Proper nutrition can be achieved through a balanced diet consisting of carbs, protein, fat, vitamins and etc. This can help your energy levels thorughout the day through breakfast, lunch and dinner, so skipping meals or not having enough nutrients from food can be detrimental to your overall health

Essential Nutrients

There are two important nutrients:

  • Macronutrients made up of (carbohydrates, proteins, fats), these are primary nutrients which are needed in large quantites to provide necessary energy
  • Micronutrients made up of (vitamins, minerals), these help to maintain the body as the various vitamins such as: vitamin A - supporting vision, immune function, vitamin C - antioxidant
  • Water

Carbohydrates can be obtained from whole grains, fruits, vegetables like bread and pasta, Proteins can be obtained from animal meat and some plants like chicken, beef, beans and tofu. Fats can be obtained through healthy oils like olive oil.

Vitamins can be obtained from fruits and vegetables, Minerals can be obtained from whole grains, meats, fruits and vegetables.

Benefits of a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet ensures that there is sufficient amounts of macro and micronutrients within the body and can help with a healthy weight and better bodily functions.

The benefits are:

  • Provides essential nutrients
  • Supports healthy growth and development
  • Helps maintain a healthy weight
  • Reduces the risk of diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers

Healthy Eating Habits

How to develop healthy habits:

  • Eat a variety of nutrient-rich foods
  • Portion control
  • Limit intake of processed foods and sugary beverages
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Read food labels and make informed choices
  • Plan meals ahead of time
